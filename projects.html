PROJECTS

Automated Solar-Powered Corn Planting Robot

Automated Solar-Powered Corn Planting Robot

Role: Programmer and documenter

Abstract

This study aims to design and create an automated robot that automatically plants corn seeds. This automated robot is also integrated with solar power to make the most of the tropical climate in the area. The Automated Solar-Powered Corn Planting Robot (ASP-CPR) was designed by the researchers with the following functions: it can drill farmed soil with a regular depth and distance and release a standard amount of corn seeds on the drilled soil automatically that follows the standard set-up of the Department of Agriculture. Upon multiple tests, results revealed that the accuracy of releasing the standard number of seeds is 94%, 98 % for the depth of drilled soil, and 92% for the distance taken by ASP-CPR between planted seeds. Additionally, the researchers integrated a notification system that notifies the user if the robot experiences low power, immovability, or an empty seed container. Moreover, the researchers conducted a product quality evaluation ISO/IEC 25010 for 20 respondents where the gathered data and results revealed an overall weighted mean of 4.13, which indicates a strong agreement amongst respondents that ASP-CPR adheres to the quality model.
